Core Positioning

Brand Strategy

Who Parshi Is For

Primary: Women aged 24–48 in Trivandrum who care about fit, want expert stitching, and are tired of boutiques that deliver late with poor quality. They have attended or will attend Kerala weddings, functions, office events, Onam celebrations.

Secondary: Brides and bridal party members in Trivandrum needing blouse + lehenga stitching 2–4 months ahead of wedding season (Oct–Jan and April–June are peak).

Brand Line (Internal)

"Parshi — Stitched with precision. Delivered with trust."

Parshi must be positioned as a reliable stitching studio with a personal touch — not a mass boutique. The North Indian master tailor is a KEY differentiator and must be woven into content and reputation. Quality + reliability (delivery on time) is what Trivandrum women complain most about. Own those two values hard.

Risk Register & Fallback Plans

Risk Management
Risk Severity Likelihood Fallback / Mitigation
Tailor leaves or falls ill High Medium Begin sourcing and vetting 1 backup tailor contact from Day 1. Build relationship with tailor so they stay (recognize them in content, fair pay, good environment). Avoid over-dependence on single tailor — train a helper in basic finishing.
Client not providing content (photos, info) High High Set clear deadlines at contract signing: content drops needed every Monday by 10am. Use stock Kerala fashion imagery as placeholder only in emergency. Create a simple WhatsApp group where client sends photos daily.
Ads not generating inquiries (Month 1) Med Medium Month 1 is learning phase — don't panic before 14 days of data. Switch creative faster if CPM is high and CTR is below 1%. Prioritize organic Instagram + GBP (free) if ads underperform early.
Instagram content not performing Med Medium Double down on Reels — they have 3–5x the organic reach of static posts in India. Before-after content consistently outperforms in fashion. Lean into customer testimonial Reels sooner if static posts lag.
Order volume increases but delivery quality drops High Medium Never over-promise delivery timelines in ads. Cap weekly new orders to what tailor can handle quality-wise. Hire assistant tailor proactively before hitting capacity — don't wait for a crisis.
Owner re-engagement / becoming bottleneck Med High Keep owner's role extremely defined from Day 1. She reviews only: monthly report (30 min), approves ad budget (5 min), handles complex customer escalations only. Agency handles everything digital. Tailor handles shop floor.
Competitor responds aggressively Low Low Trivandrum boutique market is fragmented — competition is indirect. Focus on owning your niche (precision, trust, North Indian master tailor) rather than price wars. Don't drop prices; increase perceived value through content.
Ad budget runs out mid-month Med Low Set Meta campaign budget caps. Keep a buffer of ₹1,500 not committed to any ad set. DM team monitors spend every 3 days in Month 1, weekly after that.
