Risk Register
Risks & Mitigations
Known risks to the TCGC marketing plan and how we manage them proactively.
HIGH
Content restriction on Google/Meta ads for intimate health procedures
Google and Meta restrict advertising for intimate/sexual health content. Campaigns using explicit procedure terms can be disapproved or account flagged.
✓ Mitigation: Use condition-based keywords ("post-delivery intimate care", "urinary incontinence treatment") not procedure names. Lead with educational content in ads. Build strong organic channel so paid ads are a supplement not primary driver.
HIGH
Content approval delays from Dr. Amal — slowing publishing schedule
If Dr. Amal is too busy to review and approve content in time, the 3x/week posting schedule slips, reducing reach and momentum.
✓ Mitigation: Establish weekly 30-min content review slot (fixed day/time). Batch-approve 2 weeks of content at once. Define pre-approved content themes so minor variations don't need individual approval.
MED
Website launch delayed beyond Week 5 — ads can't run without live website
Google Ads requires a live, policy-compliant website. If website is delayed, the ads service cannot launch on schedule.
✓ Mitigation: Website brief collected in Week 1. Design mockup approved by end of Week 2. Development begins Week 2. Hard deadline: Week 5 launch. Phased option: run DM-only for Month 1, ads in Month 2.
MED
Patient privacy breach risk — any social media misstep can damage trust
A single post that inadvertently compromises patient identity (even anonymised testimonials with identifiable details) could seriously damage TCGC's reputation in this sensitive specialty.
✓ Mitigation: Strict content protocol — no patient details without written consent. All testimonials reviewed by Dr. Amal before publishing. Separate "patient consent for testimonial" form. No case photos without multiple layers of consent.
MED
Low ad spend budget limiting Google Ads effectiveness
At ₹15,000/month ad spend, the daily budget is ₹500 — which may run out by noon on competitive keywords in healthcare. Insufficient data for optimisation.
✓ Mitigation: Focus ad spend on 3–4 highest-converting keywords only. Use location targeting precisely (Trivandrum + 30km radius). Build negative keyword list aggressively. Scale budget as first conversions prove ROI. Organic channel handles awareness; ads handle high-intent searches only.
LOW
Competitor response — KJK or Smart Surgeons increase their digital investment
Competitors notice TCGC's growing digital presence and respond by increasing their own content/ad spend.
✓ Mitigation: TCGC's competitive advantage is being a dedicated centre with a specialist doctor — not a department within a general hospital. Content strategy and brand positioning should consistently reinforce this differentiation. First-mover advantage in personal brand content (Dr. Amal on camera) is very difficult for institutional competitors to replicate.
LOW
Location limitation — Kanjirampara not easily accessible for all patients
Dr. Amal has noted the current location may be limiting walk-in patient volume. Digital-first patients (who search, research, then come) are less location-sensitive than walk-in patients.
✓ Mitigation: Digital marketing specifically targets pre-research patients who will travel for the right specialist. Emphasise on website: "Worth the journey — Kerala's only dedicated cosmetic gynaecology centre." If relocation happens, update GMB and website immediately — local SEO signals update within days.
